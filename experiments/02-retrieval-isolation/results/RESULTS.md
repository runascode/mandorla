## Decision

### **NO-GO**

Pair-Recall@25 lift is significantly **negative** on all three datasets (every 95% CI is well clear of zero on the wrong side of it). The PRECOMMIT decision rule requires ≥+5 pp on all three for GO, or ≥+3 pp on ≥2 of 3 with none worse than −1 pp for WEAK GO. The observed lifts are −10.65 / −6.99 / −3.23 pp. This is not a marginal miss — the intersection primitive, as operationalized, **actively degrades** gold-pair retrieval relative to the contriever baseline over the same corpus.

Per PRECOMMIT §"Decision rule": NO-GO retires the in-query intersection primitive from the active experiment queue. The retrieval form of the thesis is not pursued further with this operationalization (B2 density-extent boxes over a contriever-derived 64-D random projection, in-query only, no store).

Every secondary metric is directionally consistent with the headline: Pair-Recall@10, Any-Gold-Recall@25, and Reciprocal-Rank-of-first-pair-completion are all negative on all three datasets. There is no slice of this evaluation in which the primitive helps retrieval.

**Internal-consistency check.** HotpotQA baseline Pair-Recall@25 here is 41.39%, which matches the Experiment 01 diagnostic's "gold-pair-in-context, baseline" figure (41.39%) to the digit. The pipelines agree; the result is not an artifact of this experiment's harness.
